B2B lead generation in India is harder than it used to be. Ad costs keep rising, email deliverability is a mess, and buyers ignore cold calls. Here's what's actually working for Indian B2B businesses in 2025.

## The 7 Channels That Actually Work

### 1. LinkedIn Outreach (Smart, Not Spammy)
LinkedIn is still the #1 B2B channel for Indian decision-makers. But "Hi, my name is ___ and we help businesses…" messages get ignored.

**What works:**
- Hyper-personalized connection requests (reference their company, a post, or a recent achievement)
- Valuable content first, pitch later
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator for targeted searches
- 15–25 thoughtful outreach messages per day per rep

**Typical results:** 5–12% acceptance rate → 1–3% conversion to a call.

### 2. SEO Content Marketing
The long game. Write content targeting buyer-intent keywords:
- "[Service] company in [city]"
- "[Service] cost in India"
- "How to choose [service] provider"
- "[Competitor] vs [you]"

**Typical results:** Months 1–3 zero. Months 4–6 trickle. Months 7+ compounding pipeline.

### 3. Google Ads (High-Intent Keywords Only)
Skip broad awareness campaigns. Target:
- Bottom-of-funnel buyer terms
- Branded searches for competitors
- Specific service + city combinations
- Remarketing to site visitors

**Typical CPL:** ₹1,200–₹5,000 for B2B services in India.

### 4. WhatsApp for B2B Follow-Up
Email reply rates in India: 0.5–2%. WhatsApp reply rates: 15–30%.

Use WhatsApp for:
- Post-call follow-ups
- Sharing proposals
- Scheduling meetings
- Nurturing long-cycle leads

### 5. Cold Email (Done Right)
Email is still alive if you do it well:
- Highly targeted lists (not purchased databases)
- Personalized first line
- Single, specific ask
- Value-first, pitch-later

Typical results: 40–50% open, 5–10% reply, 1–3% to calls.

### 6. Referral Programs
Existing clients refer 2–5× better leads than cold channels. Make it systematic:
- 5–10% referral fee or credit
- Quarterly referral asks
- Named referrals (not "know anyone?")

### 7. Industry Events & Webinars
India's B2B scene is small — showing up at the right events earns disproportionate trust.

**Budget:** ₹50,000–₹5,00,000 per event; expect 20–80 leads with 2–8 qualified.

## The Channel Mix by Business Stage

**₹0–₹50 lakhs ARR:**
- 60% LinkedIn outreach
- 25% cold email
- 15% referrals

**₹50 lakhs–₹5 Cr ARR:**
- 30% LinkedIn
- 25% Google Ads
- 20% SEO content
- 15% WhatsApp
- 10% events

**₹5 Cr+ ARR:**
- 30% SEO content (now compounding)
- 25% Google Ads
- 20% events
- 15% LinkedIn
- 10% WhatsApp/referrals

## The Common Trap: Single-Channel Obsession

Indian B2B founders often go all-in on one channel because it "worked once." Then it stops working and the pipeline dies.

**Rule:** Always have 3 channels running. Never let any single channel exceed 50% of pipeline.

## Measuring What Matters

Track these weekly:
- **SQLs (sales qualified leads)** — Not MQLs. Not vanity leads.
- **Cost per SQL** by channel
- **SQL → meeting conversion**
- **Meeting → customer conversion**
- **LTV / CAC ratio**

If LTV/CAC drops below 3, your lead generation economics are broken regardless of the channel.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the cheapest B2B lead generation channel in India?**
SEO content — once it kicks in. LinkedIn is cheapest in the short term.

**Is cold email illegal in India?**
No, but spam is. Always include opt-out and respect unsubscribes.

**What tools do we use for B2B lead generation?**
Sales Navigator, Apollo, Lemlist, Zoho CRM, Wati (WhatsApp), Google Search Console.

**How many SQLs should my B2B team target per month?**
Back-calculate from revenue goal ÷ average deal size ÷ win rate. Most ₹5 Cr businesses need 40–80 SQLs/month.
