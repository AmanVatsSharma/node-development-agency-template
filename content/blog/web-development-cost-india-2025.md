## Why the Huge Range?

Website cost depends on **six factors**:

### 1. Scope & Features
A 5-page brochure site is nothing like a multi-vendor marketplace with real-time inventory. Features like user accounts, payments, multi-language, and admin dashboards multiply cost.

### 2. Technology Stack
- **WordPress + theme**: cheapest, fastest, limited flexibility
- **Shopify**: best for product-focused e-commerce
- **[Next.js/React](/pages/next-js-development)**: premium performance, great SEO, highest build quality
- **Custom Node.js backend**: needed for complex logic, integrations, real-time features

### 3. Design Quality
Template-based design: ₹0–₹25,000. Custom Figma design with brand identity work: ₹50,000–₹2,00,000.

### 4. Content
Writing, photography, videos, and translations aren't "free." Budget ₹15,000–₹75,000 for quality content.

### 5. Integrations
Payment gateways (Razorpay, Stripe), CRMs (Zoho, HubSpot), ERPs, analytics, WhatsApp, email marketing — each integration adds ₹5,000–₹30,000.

### 6. Ongoing Costs
Hosting, domain, SSL, maintenance, security updates: ₹500–₹15,000/month depending on traffic.

## What Drives Bad Quotes

Cheap quotes (under ₹25,000) almost always come with one of these traps:

- Stolen or nulled WordPress themes (security nightmare)
- No SSL, no GDPR compliance
- No proper SEO setup (missing metadata, sitemap, robots)
- "Pay more if you want it to be fast"
- No source code handover
- Hosting lock-in — you can't leave without losing the site
- No ongoing support or maintenance after launch
- No mobile optimization
- Outdated technology stack

**Rule of thumb:** If a quote is less than ₹35,000, ask exactly what's excluded.

## ROI Calculation Framework

A website investment should be evaluated on ROI, not just cost:

```
Annual Revenue from Website Conversions - Annual Operating Cost = Annual Profit
Annual Profit ÷ Initial Investment = ROI
```

**Example:**
- Website cost: ₹2,50,000
- Monthly conversions: 10 qualified leads
- Average lead value: ₹50,000
- Annual revenue: 10 × 12 × ₹50,000 = ₹60,00,000
- Annual hosting/maintenance: ₹4,00,000
- Annual profit: ₹60,00,000 - ₹4,00,000 = ₹56,00,000
- **ROI:** 2,240% in year one

A good website typically pays for itself within 1-3 months for service businesses.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why is web development cheaper in India than the US?**
Labour costs are lower, but the technical quality at top Indian agencies matches or exceeds US agencies at 1/4 the price. You're getting the same talent at a much lower cost.

**Can I build a website under ₹25,000?**
Yes — but it will be a template, not a custom build. For a serious business, expect ₹75,000+ for a real website. A cheap website is often more expensive in the long run due to lack of functionality and poor SEO.

**How much does website maintenance cost in India?**
₹3,000–₹15,000/month for basic sites; ₹25,000–₹75,000/month for e-commerce or web apps. This includes hosting, security updates, monitoring, and bug fixes.

**Is Next.js worth the premium over WordPress?**
For SEO-first, performance-critical, or custom functionality — yes. For simple blog/brochure sites — WordPress is fine. Next.js is worth it if you need speed, SEO excellence, or custom features WordPress can't do.

**What's the difference between Shopify and custom development?**
Shopify is faster to launch (2-4 weeks) and has lower initial cost (₹1-2L), but limited customization. Custom development takes longer (8-16 weeks) and costs more (₹3-15L), but gives you unlimited flexibility and ownership.

**Should I buy a pre-built theme or get a custom design?**
Pre-built themes work for new businesses on tight budgets. Custom design (₹50-2L) is worth it if you want to stand out, need specific functionality, or plan long-term growth.
