"Shopify or WooCommerce?" is the e-commerce question we hear most from Indian founders. After building stores on both for 5+ years, here's our blunt take.

## The 30-Second Answer

- **Shopify** — if you want to focus on selling, not managing. Faster to launch, easier to scale, more reliable. Costs more long-term.
- **WooCommerce** — if you already run on WordPress, have technical resources, or need unusual customization. Cheaper on paper, costlier in hidden maintenance.

For **70% of Indian e-commerce businesses**, Shopify wins. For **30%** — specifically content-heavy sites, niche marketplaces, or ultra-custom builds — WooCommerce is better.

## Real Cost Comparison for an Indian Store (1,000 orders/month)

| Cost Area | Shopify | WooCommerce |
|---|---|---|
| Platform/plan fee | ₹2,400/mo (Basic) | ₹0 |
| Hosting | Included | ₹2,000–₹8,000/mo |
| Theme (one-time) | ₹0–₹30,000 | ₹0–₹15,000 |
| Required apps/plugins | ₹4,000–₹12,000/mo | ₹2,000–₹8,000/mo |
| Developer/maintenance | ₹5,000/mo | ₹15,000–₹40,000/mo |
| Transaction fees (if not using Shopify Payments) | 2% | 0% |
| **Total monthly (realistic)** | **₹15,000–₹30,000** | **₹20,000–₹60,000** |

**Surprise:** WooCommerce often ends up **more expensive** once you factor in developer time. The "free" plugin is a myth.

## Feature-by-Feature Showdown

### Ease of Setup
- **Shopify:** Store running in 2 hours. No technical knowledge needed.
- **WooCommerce:** Requires WordPress install, hosting config, theme setup, plugin configuration. 1–5 days for a non-technical user.

**Winner:** Shopify

### Design Freedom
- **Shopify:** Good themes, but customization requires Liquid knowledge or headless approach.
- **WooCommerce:** Unlimited design flexibility via any WordPress theme.

**Winner:** WooCommerce (slightly)

### Payments in India
- **Shopify:** Razorpay, PayU, Cashfree, CCAvenue all supported. Shopify Payments not available in India yet.
- **WooCommerce:** All Indian gateways supported via plugins.

**Winner:** Tie

### SEO
- **Shopify:** Great out of the box — server-rendered, clean URLs, automatic sitemap, fast pages.
- **WooCommerce:** Depends entirely on plugins (Yoast, Rank Math) and hosting. Can be excellent or terrible.

**Winner:** Shopify (by default)

### Performance
- **Shopify:** Global CDN, 99.99% uptime, automatic scaling. Fast out of the box.
- **WooCommerce:** Performance depends on your hosting. Cheap hosting = slow site. Managed WordPress hosting matches Shopify but costs ₹4,000+/month.

**Winner:** Shopify

### App Ecosystem
- **Shopify:** 8,000+ apps. Most charge monthly. Quality curation.
- **WooCommerce:** 60,000+ plugins. Most are one-time purchase. Quality varies wildly.

**Winner:** WooCommerce for variety, Shopify for quality

### Security
- **Shopify:** PCI-compliant by default. Shopify handles security updates.
- **WooCommerce:** You are responsible for security. Outdated plugins = vulnerability.

**Winner:** Shopify

### Scalability
- **Shopify:** Handles Flash sales and 100K+ orders easily on Shopify Plus (₹2L+/month).
- **WooCommerce:** Can scale, but requires serious devops investment (Redis, Varnish, dedicated servers).

**Winner:** Shopify

## Which Store Wins for Your Use Case

**Choose Shopify if you:**
- Just want to sell products without managing infrastructure
- Plan to scale fast
- Need a store live this week
- Don't have in-house developers
- Sell in multiple countries
- Want rock-solid reliability

**Choose WooCommerce if you:**
- Already run on WordPress and have a content/blog strategy
- Need unusual product types (subscriptions, bookings, memberships)
- Have in-house WordPress developers
- Want a one-time investment rather than monthly subscriptions
- Are extremely budget-conscious and technical

## The Hidden Gotchas

**Shopify gotchas:**
- Transaction fees (2%) if you don't use Shopify Payments — and Payments isn't available in India yet
- Theme customization locked behind Liquid templating language
- Cannot self-host

**WooCommerce gotchas:**
- Plugin conflicts break stores regularly
- Security updates are **your** responsibility
- Hosting costs balloon as you grow
- "Free" quickly becomes ₹30,000–₹60,000/month once you add proper hosting, security, and support
- Slow without optimization

## Real Case Studies: When to Choose Each Platform

### Case Study 1: Shopify — Fashion Brand (100-500 products)

**Business:** Fashion accessories startup targeting Tier 1 Indian cities

**Requirements:**
- 200 SKUs initially
- Multiple variants (colors, sizes)
- Payment gateways: Razorpay, PayU
- Shipping integrations
- Email marketing automation
- Instagram shopping feed

**Decision:** Shopify

**Why:**
- Quick launch needed (4 weeks to market)
- No in-house technical team
- Expected 500% growth in year 1 (needed scalable platform)
- Could use apps instead of custom development

**Costs:**
- Setup: ₹60,000 (custom theme)
- Monthly: ₹8,000 (Shopify) + ₹5,000 (apps) + ₹3,000 (email marketing) = ₹16,000
- Developer maintenance: ₹5,000/month
- **Total: ₹21,000/month**

**Results:**
- Launched in 3 weeks
- First month: 85 orders (₹4.2L revenue)
- Month 3: 450 orders (₹22.5L revenue)
- Month 6: 1,200 orders (₹60L revenue)
- Easily scaled through Shopify without infrastructure changes

### Case Study 2: WooCommerce — Publishing/Content Platform (80 products)

**Business:** Online course + e-books platform

**Requirements:**
- Integrated blog (600+ articles)
- Membership system
- Recurring subscriptions (monthly course access)
- Student dashboard
- Progress tracking
- Extensive customization

**Decision:** WooCommerce

**Why:**
- Heavy content element (blog + courses)
- Needed deep customization (membership logic, progress tracking)
- Founder had WordPress experience
- Subscription model needed custom implementation
- Wanted to own the data

**Costs:**
- Setup: ₹1,50,000 (custom WordPress + WooCommerce development)
- Hosting (managed WordPress): ₹4,500/month
- Plugins: ₹3,000/month
- Developer (2 FTE for ongoing): ₹80,000/month
- **Total: ₹87,500/month**

**Results:**
- Complete control over student experience
- Custom membership logic impossible in Shopify
- Subscription revenue: ₹12L+/month
- Tight integration with content library
- Required ongoing maintenance (but owned it all)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is WooCommerce really free?**
The plugin is free. A production store costs ₹20,000–₹60,000/month with proper hosting, plugins, security, and maintenance.

**Which is better for SEO, Shopify or WooCommerce?**
Shopify is better out of the box. WooCommerce can match or exceed Shopify with the right plugins and hosting.

**Can I migrate from WooCommerce to Shopify?**
Yes — we do these migrations routinely. Typical timeline: 2–4 weeks.

**What's the cheapest way to start an online store in India?**
Shopify Basic at ₹2,400/month with a free theme is the fastest, cheapest path to a professional store.

**Should I start with WooCommerce and upgrade to Shopify later?**
Generally no. Migration costs ₹1-2L and takes 4-6 weeks. Better to choose the right platform upfront.

**What if I need a marketplace (multiple sellers)?**
WooCommerce with Dokan/Multivendor plugins is better. Shopify isn't designed for marketplaces.

**Can I run both Shopify and WooCommerce simultaneously?**
Yes, some brands do. You'd have two separate stores and sync inventory manually. More complexity and cost.

**What about Wix, BigCommerce, or other platforms?**
Shopify and WooCommerce dominate India (70%+ market share combined). Others exist but are riskier bets.
