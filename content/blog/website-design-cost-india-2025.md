**What "quality" means:** custom design by a real designer, conversion-focused UX, mobile-first, proper SEO, measurable results.

## The 5 Components of Website Cost

### 1. UX / Wireframing — 15% of budget
User flows, wireframes, information architecture. Skipping this is the #1 reason websites fail to convert.

### 2. Visual Design — 30% of budget
Custom Figma designs, brand identity, typography, color systems, illustrations, photography.

### 3. Development — 40% of budget
Converting designs to actual code, responsive layouts, animations, CMS integration, testing.

### 4. Content — 10% of budget
Copywriting, images, videos. Often overlooked — results in empty pages and weak SEO.

### 5. Technical Setup — 5% of budget
Hosting, domain, SSL, analytics, SEO setup, submission to Google Search Console.

## Website Designs That Actually Convert

### Key Conversion Optimizations Worth Paying For

1. **A/B testing the hero section**
   - Too many designers waste hero space on big animations
   - Real conversion winners: Clear headline + CTA + trust indicators
   - Cost to implement: ₹10–15K, ROI: 20–30% better conversions

2. **Strategic CTA placement**
   - CTAs above the fold, mid-page, bottom of page
   - Different CTAs for different segments (pricing vs features vs demo)
   - Cost: ₹5–10K, ROI: 15–25% more conversions

3. **Social proof section**
   - Real customer testimonials, not stock photos
   - Customer logos, review counts, trust badges
   - Cost: ₹10–20K (for good testimonial video), ROI: 10–20% lift

4. **Mobile-first design**
   - 70–80% of Indian traffic is mobile
   - Desktop-first sites built for phones are disaster
   - Cost to do right: ₹20–40K extra, ROI: 100%+ (doubles mobile conversions)

5. **Performance optimization**
   - LCP < 1.5 seconds is table stakes in 2025
   - Slow sites lose 20% of conversions per second delay
   - Cost: ₹15–30K (images, code-splitting, CDN), ROI: Massive

| Factor | WordPress (with premium theme) | Custom-Built (Next.js) |

## What Kills Website Projects

1. **Scope creep** — "While we're designing, let's add..."
   - Solution: Freeze scope at start, add features in v2

2. **Content delays** — "We'll write copy during design"
   - Solution: Have copy BEFORE design starts

3. **Too many stakeholders** — 10 people giving feedback = chaos
   - Solution: 1–2 decision makers, feedback consolidation

4. **Perfectionism** — "This doesn't look quite right"
   - Solution: Ship MVP, optimize based on data

5. **Poor handoff** — "Here's the design, hire a developer"
   - Solution: Designer and developer should collaborate

6. **No testing** — Launch without testing on real devices/networks
   - Solution: Test on slow 4G network, actual devices

7. **Ignoring analytics** — Launch then forget
   - Solution: Set up GA4, track conversions, optimize monthly

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What's the minimum I should spend on a professional website in India?**
₹75,000–₹1,00,000 is the practical floor for custom-designed, properly SEO'd, conversion-focused websites. Less than that is usually a template with minimal customization.

**Why is WordPress cheaper than Next.js?**
WordPress has a massive ecosystem of cheap templates and plugins. Next.js requires real developers to build custom. But Next.js delivers 3–5x better conversion rates over time, so ROI is better.

**How long does website design take in India?**
- Simple business site: 3–4 weeks
- Custom e-commerce: 6–10 weeks
- SaaS platform + documentation: 10–16 weeks
- Enterprise platform: 16–24 weeks

**Can I negotiate website design costs?**
Yes — but do it right. Reduce scope (fewer pages, fewer features, simpler integrations), don't reduce quality. A cheap website that converts poorly is more expensive than a quality website.

**Should I hire a freelancer or agency for website design?**
**Freelancer:** Good for simple sites, faster, cheaper. Risk: May lack specialization.
**Agency:** Better for complex projects, has backups, quality controls. Cost: Higher.
**Hybrid:** Use freelancer for design, agency for development (or vice versa).

**What about no-code platforms (Webflow, Framer)?**
Good for: Simple landing pages, portfolios, brochures
Not good for: E-commerce, SaaS, complex integrations, scaling
Truth: Cheaper upfront (₹30–80K), but limited long-term.
